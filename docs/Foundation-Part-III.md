# Part III — Future Research

The preceding parts introduced **Temporal Liquidity** as an economic concept and developed a conceptual framework for decentralized execution markets.

This Foundation Statement deliberately focuses on concepts rather than protocol mechanisms. Its objective is not to prescribe a particular protocol architecture, but to identify a research direction motivated by the continuing evolution of Ethereum protocol economics.

Building upon advances in fee market design, Proposer-Builder Separation (PBS), Enshrined PBS (ePBS), execution markets, and related protocol research, this final part outlines several questions that naturally emerge from the framework developed in the preceding sections.

These questions are intentionally open and are offered as invitations for further investigation rather than conclusions.

---

# 9. Mechanism Design Space

One of the central ideas developed in this Foundation Statement is the separation between **economic concepts** and **protocol mechanisms**.

Temporal Liquidity is proposed as an economic property describing the flexibility of demand with respect to execution time. It should not be identified with any particular protocol implementation.

Ethereum's evolution demonstrates that multiple mechanisms may address similar economic objectives. EIP-1559, PBS, ePBS, Execution Tickets, Slot Auctions, and future protocol proposals each explore different aspects of decentralized market coordination.

Within the TLM framework, future mechanisms may be evaluated according to how effectively they represent and coordinate Temporal Liquidity while preserving decentralization, neutrality, simplicity, and market efficiency.

This Foundation Statement intentionally leaves those implementation questions open.

---

# 10. Measuring Temporal Liquidity

Recent empirical studies suggest that execution delay possesses measurable economic value for many blockchain applications.

These observations motivate a broader question.

If transactions exhibit different degrees of flexibility with respect to execution time, can **Temporal Liquidity** itself be measured?

Future work may investigate:

* empirical estimation of Temporal Liquidity across different transaction classes;
* simulation methodologies for decentralized execution markets;
* metrics describing temporal characteristics of applications;
* evaluation methodologies for temporal protocol mechanisms.

Developing quantitative methodologies will be an important step toward evaluating future proposals objectively.

---

# 11. Protocol Evolution

Ethereum protocol development continues to evolve through ongoing research into fee markets, PBS, ePBS, execution rights, protocol coordination, and related areas.

This Foundation Statement does not propose an alternative roadmap.

Instead, it offers a conceptual framework through which these developments may be interpreted from the perspective of decentralized execution markets and Temporal Liquidity.

Whether future protocol evolution ultimately incorporates richer temporal representations remains an open research question.

TLM simply proposes that execution time represents an economically meaningful dimension worthy of further investigation.

---

# 12. Non-goals

To clarify the scope of this work, several objectives intentionally fall outside this Foundation Statement.

Specifically, TLM is **not**:

* a new consensus protocol;
* a replacement for PBS or existing Ethereum protocol architecture;
* a proposal for a single protocol mechanism;
* an attempt to maximize execution speed or minimize latency alone;
* a complete solution to decentralized execution markets.

Instead, this work proposes a conceptual framework for studying one additional economic dimension of decentralized execution: **Temporal Liquidity**.

---

# 13. Open Questions

Many important questions remain unresolved.

Examples include:

* How should Temporal Liquidity be represented within blockchain protocols?
* Which temporal information, if any, should become protocol-visible?
* Can Temporal Liquidity be measured empirically across different applications?
* How should protocol simplicity be balanced against richer market expressiveness?
* What new incentive or strategic behaviors might emerge?
* How should future protocol mechanisms be evaluated?

These questions are expected to evolve as both Ethereum and TLM continue to mature.

---

# 14. Conclusion

Ethereum has made remarkable progress in decentralized protocol economics through continuing advances in fee market design, decentralized block construction, execution markets, and protocol coordination.

This Foundation Statement seeks to build upon that progress.

Rather than proposing a single mechanism, it introduces **Temporal Liquidity** as a candidate economic property describing the flexibility of demand with respect to execution time and develops a conceptual framework for investigating how decentralized execution markets may coordinate richer forms of economic information.

Whether Temporal Liquidity ultimately becomes protocol-visible, and how it should be represented within future protocols, remain open questions.

The objective of this Foundation Statement is therefore not to conclude the discussion, but to begin it.

TLM is offered as a contribution to the ongoing evolution of Ethereum research, with the hope that future collaboration, empirical study, and protocol experimentation will further deepen our understanding of decentralized execution markets.
